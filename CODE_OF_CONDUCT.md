# Code of Conduct

## Purpose

This Code of Conduct establishes the standards of behavior expected from all contributors, maintainers, and community members participating in the Friendlydll project. Violations will be met with immediate and decisive action.

## Scope

This policy applies to all project spaces including but not limited to: the repository, issues, pull requests, discussions, Discord servers, direct messages related to the project, and any public space where an individual is representing the project.

## Standards

### Required Behavior

1. **Respect all participants.** Treat every contributor, user, and community member with dignity regardless of experience level, identity, background, or perspective.
2. **Communicate constructively.** Criticism must target code, ideas, or approaches -- never people. Provide actionable feedback.
3. **Accept responsibility.** If you make a mistake, own it, apologize, and fix it. Do not deflect blame or make excuses.
4. **Respect privacy.** Never share private communications, personal information, or identifying details of any community member without explicit consent.
5. **Credit contributions.** Acknowledge the work of others. Do not claim credit for code, ideas, or discoveries that are not yours.
6. **Follow the security policy.** Report vulnerabilities privately per `SECURITY.md`. Never disclose security issues publicly before a fix is released.
7. **Keep discussions on topic.** Project spaces are for project-related discussion. Take unrelated conversations elsewhere.

### Prohibited Behavior

The following will result in immediate action with no warnings:

1. **Harassment**: Unwelcome comments, slurs, personal attacks, intimidation, stalking, unwanted sexual attention, or sustained disruption of discussions. Zero tolerance.
2. **Discrimination**: Derogatory remarks or exclusionary behavior based on race, ethnicity, nationality, religion, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, age, or any other protected characteristic.
3. **Doxxing**: Publishing or threatening to publish any private or personally identifying information (real names, addresses, emails, IPs, photos, financial information, social media accounts) without explicit consent.
4. **Threats**: Any threat of violence, harm, legal action used as intimidation, swatting, or any form of real-world retaliation.
5. **Weaponization**: Using the software, its techniques, or knowledge gained from this project to target, harass, stalk, or cause real-world harm to any individual.
6. **Distribution of malware**: Submitting pull requests, releases, or builds that contain backdoors, keyloggers, credential stealers, ransomware, remote access trojans, cryptocurrency miners, or any payload beyond the stated functionality of the project.
7. **Impersonation**: Misrepresenting your identity, affiliation, or authority within the project.
8. **Brigading**: Coordinating groups to harass, mass-report, or disrupt other communities or individuals.
9. **Sabotage**: Intentionally introducing bugs, vulnerabilities, or destructive code into the project.
10. **Spamming**: Unsolicited advertising, self-promotion, or repetitive low-quality contributions.
11. **Circumventing bans**: Creating alternate accounts or using proxies to evade enforcement actions.

### Contribution Standards

1. **No backdoors.** Every line of code must do what it claims to do. Hidden functionality that compromises the user's system, exfiltrates data, or grants unauthorized access to third parties is grounds for permanent ban and public disclosure of the offending code.
2. **No credential harvesting.** The project must never collect, store, or transmit Steam credentials, auth tokens, passwords, or session data. Contributions that introduce such functionality will be rejected and the contributor banned.
3. **No targeting individuals.** Features must be general-purpose. Contributions designed to target specific players, servers, or communities by name, SteamID, or IP will be rejected.
4. **No destructive payloads.** Features that cause permanent data loss, hardware damage, or denial of service beyond the scope of normal gameplay interaction are prohibited.
5. **Test your code.** Do not submit untested contributions. Broken code that crashes the host process or corrupts game state wastes everyone's time.

## Enforcement

### Reporting

Report violations to **the404studios@gmail.com** with:
- Description of the incident
- Links or screenshots as evidence
- Identities of those involved (usernames, not real names unless relevant)
- Any additional context

All reports will be reviewed confidentially. The reporter's identity will not be disclosed to the accused without consent.

### Actions

Enforcement is at the sole discretion of the project maintainers. Actions are proportional to severity but err on the side of protecting the community:

| Severity | Action |
|----------|--------|
| **Minor** (first-offense rudeness, off-topic spam, low-quality contributions) | Written warning with specific behavioral expectations. Second offense escalates to temporary ban. |
| **Moderate** (repeated minor violations, targeted insults, bad-faith contributions, ignoring warnings) | Temporary ban (7-30 days) from all project spaces. Duration at maintainer discretion. |
| **Severe** (harassment, discrimination, doxxing threats, malware distribution, sabotage) | Permanent ban from all project spaces. No appeal. Offending contributions purged from history. |
| **Critical** (actual doxxing, real-world threats, backdoor insertion, credential harvesting) | Permanent ban. Public disclosure of the incident (without victim details). Report to platform trust & safety. Law enforcement referral if applicable. |

### Appeals

- **Warnings**: No formal appeal. Demonstrate improved behavior.
- **Temporary bans**: May be appealed once via email after the ban period expires. Include what you did, why it was wrong, and what you will do differently.
- **Permanent bans**: No appeal for severe or critical violations. Permanent means permanent.

### Maintainer Accountability

Maintainers are held to the same standards as all other participants. If a maintainer violates this Code of Conduct, report the incident to another maintainer or, if none are available, document the violation publicly in a new repository issue tagged `[CoC Violation - Maintainer]`.

Maintainers who abuse their enforcement powers (retaliatory bans, selective enforcement, using moderation to silence legitimate criticism) will be removed from their role.
